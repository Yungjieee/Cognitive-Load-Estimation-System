About CLES

What is CLES?

The Problem

Many learners feel overwhelmed when solving difficult multi-step learning tasks, which can lead to errors, frustration, and giving up.

Our Solution

CLES estimates cognitive load in real-time during practice sessions and provides timely support plus comprehensive feedback reports to help learners improve.

Impact

Key Benefits

Reduces Overload

Prevents cognitive burnout by detecting high load early and providing timely intervention

Increases Self-Awareness

Real-time feedback helps learners understand their cognitive state and stress levels

Builds Better Study Strategy

Personalized insights and SWOT analysis guide improvement across topics

  • Help-Seeking - Hints viewed, examples shown
  • User Profile - Prior knowledge, experience level
  • Environment - Noise rating and distractions

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Cognitive load refers to the amount of mental effort and working memory resources required to process information and complete a task. When cognitive load is too high, learners become overwhelmed and struggle to understand or retain information. CLES measures this load in real-time using physiological signals (heart rate variability), behavioral indicators (attention and help-seeking), and self-reported data (NASA-TLX survey) to help optimize learning experiences and prevent cognitive overload.

Currently, CLES focuses on Data Structures topics including Arrays, Linked Lists, Stacks, Queues, Sorting algorithms, and Trees. However, the platform is designed to be content-agnostic and can be extended to other subjects like mathematics, programming languages, or any problem-solving domain. We're actively expanding the content library.

After completing multiple sessions across different topics, Gemini AI analyzes your performance data, cognitive load patterns, and learning behaviors to generate a comprehensive SWOT analysis. It identifies your Strengths (topics you excel at), Weaknesses (areas needing improvement), Opportunities (potential growth areas), and Threats (challenges that may hinder progress). This provides actionable insights for personalized learning paths.
